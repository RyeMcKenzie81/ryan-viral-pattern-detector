## Your Research Questions

### 1. What Do Competitors Get Wrong?
Look for:
- Outdated advice
- Generic platitudes without specifics
- Missing context (e.g., assumes all readers are the same)
- Wrong assumptions about the topic
- Overly academic/formal tone that alienates readers

List 3-5 specific mistakes or weaknesses.

### 2. What's Missing Completely?
Identify gaps:
- Questions they don't answer
- Perspectives they ignore
- Practical examples they lack
- Follow-up advice they skip
- Real pain points they miss

List 3-5 major gaps.

### 3. What's YOUR Unique Angle?
Based on your experience:
- What have you learned that gives you a different perspective?
- What advice do you give that contradicts common wisdom?
- What story can you tell that proves your point?
- What does {BRAND_NAME} enable that other solutions don't?

Write 2-3 paragraphs explaining your unique take.

### 4. What Questions Are Readers ACTUALLY Asking?
Think about:
- Comments on competitor articles
- Reddit and forum threads
- Questions people ask directly
- Follow-up questions after reading existing content

List 5-10 specific questions that need answering.
