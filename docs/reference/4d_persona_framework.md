# 4D Persona Framework

A comprehensive framework for building deep customer personas that drive effective copywriting. Use this to understand your target customer (or competitor's customer) at a level that enables resonant messaging.

## The 4 Dimensions

1. **Demographics & Behavior** - Who they are externally
2. **Psychographics & Desires** - What they want internally
3. **Identity & Social** - How they see themselves and relate to others
4. **Worldview & Domain** - How they interpret reality and your product category

---

## Dimension 1: Persona Basics

### Snapshot
A 2-3 sentence big picture description of this person.

### Demographics
- Age range
- Gender
- Location/geography
- Income level
- Education
- Occupation
- Family status

### Behavior & Habits / In the Life
- Daily routines
- Media consumption habits
- How they spend free time
- Work/life balance
- Health habits

### Digital Presence
- Primary social platforms
- Content consumption patterns
- Online shopping behavior
- Device preferences
- Time spent online

### Product & Purchase Drivers
- What triggers them to seek solutions
- How they research purchases
- Price sensitivity
- Brand loyalty patterns
- Impulse vs. considered purchases

### Cultural Context
- Cultural background
- Regional influences
- Generational identity
- Subcultures they belong to

### Likely Typology Profile
- MBTI tendencies
- Enneagram patterns
- DISC profile indicators
- Other personality frameworks

## Dimension 2: Psychographic Mapping

### Transformation Map

| BEFORE (Current State) | AFTER (Desired State) |
|------------------------|----------------------|
| Current frustrations | Desired relief |
| Current limitations | Desired capabilities |
| Current identity | Aspirational identity |
| Current emotions | Desired emotions |

### The 10 Core Desires

When analyzing ads or building personas, identify which desires are being appealed to and capture the specific verbiage used:

#### 1. Survival, Enjoyment of Life, Life Extension
- Living longer, healthier
- Avoiding death, disease, disability
- Vitality and energy

#### 2. Enjoyment of Food and Beverages
- Taste, pleasure, satisfaction
- Social eating experiences
- Food as comfort

#### 3. Freedom from Fear, Pain, Worry, Regret, Past Failures, Shame and Guilt
- Escape from negative emotions
- Peace of mind
- Forgiveness (self and others)
- Moving past mistakes

#### 4. Sexual Companionship
- Romantic connection
- Physical attraction
- Intimacy and closeness

#### 5. Comfortable Living Conditions
- Home comfort
- Financial security
- Convenience and ease

#### 6. Superiority, Admiration, Winning, Status
- Being the best
- Recognition and respect
- Competitive advantage
- Social status markers

#### 7. Care and Protection of Loved Ones
- Family safety
- Providing for others
- Being a good parent/partner/friend

#### 8. Social Approval / Being Seen
- Fitting in
- Being accepted
- Having a voice
- Being understood

#### 9. Justice / Righting Unfairness
- Making things right
- Punishing wrongdoers
- Fairness and equality

#### 10. Self-Actualization
- Reaching potential
- Personal growth
- Meaning and purpose
- Legacy

## Dimension 3: Identity & Social

### Identity Questions

**Self-Narratives**: What stories do they tell themselves about themselves?
- "Because I am X, therefore I Y"
- "I'm the kind of person who..."
- "People like me don't..."

**Current Self-Image**: How do they describe themselves in their own words?

**Past Failures**: What have they tried that didn't work? Who do they blame?

**Desired Self-Image**:
- Their wildest dreams they won't share with others
- How they want to be seen by the world
- How they want to be recognized

**Identity Artifacts**: What objects, brands, or symbols are associated with their desired image?

### Social Dynamics / Relations

Map the people in their life by relationship type:

| Relationship Type | Who | Why It Matters |
|------------------|-----|----------------|
| People They Admire | | Role models, aspirational |
| People They Envy | | What they secretly want |
| People They Want to Impress | | Motivational drivers |
| People They Love/Loyal To | | Protective instincts |
| People They Dislike | | Tribal identity markers |
| People They Feel Compared To | | Competitive dynamics |
| People Who Influence Purchases | | Decision influencers |
| People They Fear Being Judged By | | Risk aversion triggers |
| People They Want to Belong To | | Aspiration groups |
| People They Want Distance From | | Negative identity markers |

## Dimension 4: Worldview & Domain Sentiment

### Worldview

**Reality Interpretation**: How do they see the world? What's their general worldview?

**World Stories**: What narratives do they believe?
- Cause and effect beliefs
- Goals and obstacles
- Heroes and villains
- Oppressors and oppressed

**Core Convictions**: What are their fundamental beliefs and values?

**Forces of Good**: What do they believe stands for good? What IS good?

**Forces of Evil**: What do they believe represents deception, dishonesty, immorality?

**Cultural Zeitgeist**: What era/moment do they believe they're living in?

**Allergies**: What triggers negative reactions? What are they "allergic" to in messaging?

### Domain Sentiment (Product-Specific)

**Outcome/JTBD/Transformation**:
- Emotional outcomes desired
- Social outcomes desired
- Functional outcomes desired

**Pain Points**:
- Emotional pain points
- Social pain points
- Functional pain points

**Desired Features**: What specific features do they want?

**Failed Solutions**: What have they tried that didn't work?

**Buying Objections**:
- Emotional objections ("I'm afraid...")
- Social objections ("What will people think...")
- Functional objections ("Will it actually work...")

**Familiar Promises**: What claims have they heard before and are skeptical of?

## Additional Insights

### Purchase Behavior
- **Pain Symptoms**: Observable signs of the pain points
- **Activation Events**: What triggers them to buy NOW?
- **Purchasing Habits**: How they typically buy
- **Decision Process**: Steps they go through
- **Current Workarounds**: Hacks they use instead of buying

### 3D Objections
- **Emotional Risks**: What they're afraid of feeling
- **Barriers to Behavior**: What stops them from taking action

## Using 4D Personas for Copy

### Hook Creation
- Appeal to specific desires (Dimension 2)
- Reference identity narratives (Dimension 3)
- Align with worldview (Dimension 4)

### Body Copy
- Use their actual language/verbiage
- Address specific pain points
- Paint the transformation (before â†’ after)

### Objection Handling
- Address emotional risks
- Handle social concerns
- Prove functional claims

### Call to Action
- Leverage activation events
- Remove barriers to behavior
- Appeal to desired identity

## Competitive Analysis Application

When analyzing competitor ads, extract:
1. What desires they're appealing to (and the exact words)
2. What identity they're targeting
3. What social dynamics they're leveraging
4. What worldview they assume
5. What pain points they address
6. What transformation they promise

This reveals their positioning and target customer, enabling differentiation.
